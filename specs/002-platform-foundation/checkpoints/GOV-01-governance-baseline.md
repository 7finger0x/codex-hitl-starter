## Constitution gap analysis

The current constitution contains seven articles:

1. Specification precedes implementation.
2. Human judgment controls irreversible decisions.
3. Verification is mandatory.
4. Scope is traceable.
5. Deviations are explicit.
6. Context is repository-owned.
7. Autonomy increases through evidence.

Final Production Paper v3 requires at least ten articles. The current constitution
does not state full-capability/no-arbitrary-cap rules, deterministic control before
generative judgment, structured safe command execution, tenant/secret/data
isolation, reversible delivery, or no-silent-bypass with sufficient precision.

## Proposed constitution amendment

**Proposed version**: 2.0.0  
**Version rationale**: The amendment expands and reorders governance principles
and introduces new mandatory execution, tenancy, recovery, and visibility
constraints. A major version makes the migration explicit.

### Article I - Specification before implementation

Material behavior must be defined in an approved specification with measurable
acceptance criteria and an implementation plan before implementation. Durable
context belongs in version-controlled repository artifacts, not only transcripts.

### Article II - Full capability without arbitrary architecture limits

The architecture must support the approved product scope without hidden,
hard-coded exclusions or undocumented vendor, asset, provider, model, chain, or
execution caps. Operational exposure is governed through tenant, environment,
resource, role, configuration, and policy controls.

### Article III - Deterministic control before generative judgment

Authorization, policy precedence, state transitions, risk limits, token
validation, tenant boundaries, idempotency, and command admission must be
deterministic, schema-validated, side-effect controlled, and fail closed. AI output
cannot replace these controls.

### Article IV - Human authority for governed actions

Destructive, externally visible, security-sensitive, financially material,
scope-expanding, or otherwise checkpointed actions require attributable approval
from an authorized principal for the exact action and reviewed artifact state.
Agents cannot invent, infer, reuse, or self-issue approval.

### Article V - Safe command execution

Commands must use structured argument arrays, shell=False semantics where a
subprocess API is used, controlled working directories, explicit executable and
path validation, allowlisted environment handling, bounded input/output/time and
resources, process-tree termination, and recorded sanitized results.

### Article VI - Traceability

Every mandatory requirement must link to approved tasks, implementation artifacts,
automated tests, acceptance checks, evidence, and the exact source revision.
Missing, duplicate, malformed, or dangling mandatory links block completion.

### Article VII - Evidence before completion

Success, readiness, and completion claims must be generated from objective,
machine-readable checks bound to source, policy, configuration, dependency, and
working-tree identity. Failed, skipped, degraded, truncated, unavailable, or
approval-gated checks cannot be reported as passing. Autonomy may increase only
through explicit policy change supported by repeated evidence.

### Article VIII - Tenant, secret, and data isolation

Every platform and engineering boundary must preserve tenant, environment,
identity, credential, and data classification. Secrets and raw environment values
must not enter logs, traces, errors, AI context, analytics, exports, fixtures, or
ordinary records. Development tooling cannot bypass platform privacy or tenancy.

### Article IX - Reversible delivery

Every material change must include rollback or tested forward recovery
proportional to impact. Compatibility must be preserved across rolling changes.
Destructive or irreversible work requires separate explicit approval and evidence.

### Article X - No silent bypass

Failures, exceptions, policy overrides, degraded verification, truncation,
unavailable dependencies, unsupported claims, and temporary deviations must be
visible in output and immutable evidence. Controls, scanners, tests, and policy
gates cannot be silently disabled or bypassed.
